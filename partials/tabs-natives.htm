Having a garden that is a welcoming place for butterflies can be a real joy. There are a number of butterflies that are native to our region, and one way to be able to best attract them is by planting natives.

We've provided a list of many of the NW Native plants that we see during the year and that are good butterfly plants as well as good garden plants. Read about the plants>>

Hummingbird gardens come in every shape and size, from large landscapes to containers on a patio. These tiny birds are joyful to watch – consider planting something to bring them into your garden this year!

With a little planning using our Hummingbird Feature, you can be sure to provide something in bloom for these delightful birds throughout the season. Read about the plants>>

The wild fruit of the Pacific Northwest offers a variety of fresh and delicious flavors. They can be used alone or combined with other fruit for a unique twist in your favorite jam, sauce or baking recipes.

Include these plants in your ‘edible forest’ and have a bounty that you can both enjoy and share with the birds that visit your garden! Read about the plants>>
